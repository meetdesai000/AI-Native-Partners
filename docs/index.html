Unsure if AI Can Be Trusted in Your Field? You're Not Wrong.

Back in your student days, you were taught:

"A computer only does what it's programmed to do."

Machine learning didn't change that. It just changed what we mean by a "program."

Today, data is the program — and for that to work, it needs to be:

  • Enough
  • Clean
  • Labeled

When AI underdelivers, it's usually because one (or more) of these data conditions isn't met — especially in high-stakes fields like healthcare, finance, policy, or law. Your hesitation isn't old-school. It's practical.

Afraid of AI? Don't Be.

To be in awe of machines that predict without explicit instructions is to forget: They're still just optimizing functions — minimizing error in their predictions.

And to fear machines becoming sentient? That's just us getting spooked by our own reflection — or a Hollywood script.

We humans have much to be humble about. So do the machines.

So What Has AI Actually Learned?

In other words — where have the data conditions been met?

Text. Books, blogs, wikis, chats, codebases.

That's why Large Language Models (LLMs) are great at predicting what fits a sentence:

  • Not reality.
  • Not context.
  • Just coherent next steps in language.

On their own, that's not very useful beyond grammar checks. But give a smart team API access... and they'll bend that into something powerful.
